Leben, Entropy et Mémoire ^

Just because something bears the aspect of the inevitable one should not, therefore, go along willingly with it.

What's a living thing? One can define Life in many ways only soon to find out that some things don't fit that definition very well and yet one will see them alive. One thing, though, seems to be common to all of them: entropy. I believe there is another way of seeing what life is and that is through the concept of memory.

My premise is very simple. There is no future. There is no past. The present is an illusion or at the very least quite ephemeral, ungraspable. When it comes to the human brain, its capacity is limited. It can't process all the information at once which means the present, to the brain, is reduced to a few microseconds. I don't know if the brain has the capacity to do some parallel processing but even if that's the case the limit exists at some level (and remember that we only process what the eyes see which is not 100% what's happening). The only way to move through time is by having some sort of record. In living things, DNA. In higher layer of consciousness, memory. This, I pressume, affects every thing in our brains including language. I find interesting that some languages don't have the concept of gerund (they're be-ing). Things are or will be but they are not on going. Some other languages associate time with space direction. Fascinating.

To be is only possible through the eyes of others. Reality is a relation. Memory is a relation between two points. Entropy is the many relations between spacetime points. And Life seems to be negative entropy that is only possible we keep track of things.
